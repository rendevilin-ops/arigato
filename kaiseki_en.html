Kaiseki Menu – Arigato

The “Kaiseki menu” represents one of the most refined forms of Japanese cuisine. Originally served during important meetings, receptions or diplomatic gatherings, it is designed to accompany conversation, sharing and harmony between guests.

A culinary journey in several acts

Kaiseki is composed of a sequence of seasonal dishes, each crafted to create a rhythm, an atmosphere and a specific emotion. Below is the traditional structure of our Kaiseki menu:

The 10 courses of a Kaiseki meal

  • 1. Sakizuke — Appetizer
    Ex: sesame tofu, seasonal vegetables
  • 2. Hassun — Assortment to accompany sake
    Ex: small seasonal bites
  • 3. Wan-mono — Broth-based dish
    Ex: clear soup, fish dumpling
  • 4. Mukozuke — Sashimi
    Ex: tuna, sea bream, salmon
  • 5. Nimono — Simmered dish
    Ex: simmered vegetables, Japanese ravioli
  • 6. Yakimono — Grilled dish
    Ex: grilled yakitori, light marinade
  • 7. Agemono — Fried dish
    Ex: tempura, crab croquette
  • 8. Sunomono / Shokuji — Sushi or refreshing course
    Ex: salmon, sea bream, shrimp
  • 9. Suimono — Miso soup
    Ex: bonito broth, red miso
  • 10. Mizugashi — Dessert
    Ex: seasonal fruits, wagashi

The spirit of Kaiseki

More than a simple meal, Kaiseki tells a story: a gentle beginning, a rise in flavors, a refreshing pause, then a calm conclusion with rice, soup and dessert. Each dish carries intention and care for the guest.
